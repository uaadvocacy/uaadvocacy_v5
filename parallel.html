Meeting the Local, Regional, and National Needs of a Global Marketplace
To address the need for greater access to higher education, UA Online, the University of Arizona's virtual campus, offers the hallmark UA educational experience and student opportunities to a growing segment of the population - the increasing number of students who prefer the flexibility an online learning environment provides. In the rankings released by the 2017 U.S. News & World Report Best Online Programs briefing, the University of Arizona debuted in the top 25% of all programs nationwide. With 30 undergraduate degree offerings, and over 40 graduate degrees and certificates available for enrollment, UA Online offers a fully-supported educational model, with in-house platform design, content review, and course management. This unique environment allows the UA to deliver the same high-quality, in-person educational experience online as well as a variety of supplemental programs for professional development and secondary education use. The 2017 U.S. News & World Report acknowledged multiple online graduate programs, such as ranking the Eller College of Management online MBA as No. 55 out of 179 and its master's in MIS as No. 11 out of 38. The UA nursing graduate program also placed No. 42 out of 148.
High School Dual Enrollment: Ethics, Economy, and Entrepreneurship
This course, Ethics, Economy, and Entrepreneurship, has been developed for high school students interested in going beyond the basic state standards for economics. Students complete the course better prepared for college and for the ethical challenges that accompany a variety of careers in business, law, politics, education, or journalism. The curriculum for this course was developed as a collaboration between individual Tucson high school teachers and University of Arizona faculty. Students enrolled in this course can apply to the University of Arizona and receive 3 academic credits upon successful course completion. These academic credits may be applied towards a future degree at the University of Arizona, offering substantial savings based on current tuition rates.
McGuire Center's Business Certificate Program for Aspiring Business Owners
In Spring 2017, 140 community leaders in Southern Arizona completed the McGuire Center's Business Certificate. The certificate, which is a new offering from the Eller College of Management, is a six-week course in management, marketing, sales, accounting, finance, and legal issues. An effort to jump-start the regional economy through increased access to education, the certificate program is offered in English in the Fall, and in Spanish in the Spring. Of the 140 participants who completed the Spring certificate program, 138 were Hispanic and 107 were women. Although half of the participants were currently operating a business while enrolled in the program, the other half intend to select entrepreneurial pursuits in the near future.
