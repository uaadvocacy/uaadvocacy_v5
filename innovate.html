Founded in 1976, the University of Arizona Cancer Center (UACC) is a Center of Excellence in the Arizona Health Sciences Center. In 1990, the Center was designated as one of the first comprehensive cancer centers in the nation by the National Cancer Institute (NCI) and remains the only such center in Arizona.

We are the innovators.

Diabetic Foot Research Partnership with Cuba
University of Arizona Health Sciences physician-scientists are pursuing a groundbreaking partnership with researchers in Cuba to steward FDA approval of a revolutionary diabetic foot ulcer drug currently not available for prescription in the United States. Developed at the Cuban Center for Genetic Engineering and Biotechnology, the drug, Heberprot-P, underw ent clinical trials in 2006 and is currently available for use in over 25 countries. Heberprot-P has resulted in rapid wound healing as well as a 75 percent reduction in amputations and a longer and better quality of life for patients suffering from diabetic foot ulcers.
Launch of the Transportation Research Institute
In 2016, the UA opened the Transportation Research Institute (TRI) as a hub for interdisciplinary collaboration to tackle the challenges of an ever-evolving transportation ecosystem. With renowned partnerships expertise from associate director Ann Wilkey, TRI is poised to equip the State of Arizona, the second state to authorize legislation for autonomous vehicle testing, with the planning strategy and technical tools to create economic growth while leading efforts to address a national challenge. Current negotiations with the Ambassador from Costa Rica to the U.S., Roman Macaya, are underway to enhance the UA's international collaboration efforts. Through the College of Engineering, the U.S. Department of Transportation awarded the TRI a $15.6M multi-university award to improve the mobility of people and objects.
Launch of BAS in Cyber Operations at UA South
In Fall 2016, the University of Arizona South formally launched the on-campus Bachelor of Applied Science (BAS) in Cyber Operations, with the online option slated for open enrollment in Fall 2017. The program was conceived and developed as part of a larger collaborative effort to establish Sierra Vista as a regional hub of cybersecurity education, industry, and research. A joint-effort between UA South and the City of Sierra Vista, the new degree program was designed with input from defense experts at Raytheon, Northrop Grumman, Lockheed Martin, and Science Foundation Arizona to support workforce development, job creation, and the infrastructure necessary for cyber research and development in Southern Arizona. The program has been approved by the Arizona Department of Veteran Services State Approving Agency (SAA), allowing enrolled veteran students to utilize their educational benefits.
